Three ways to add neon green + purple

Same content, same fonts (Fraunces / Oswald / JetBrains Mono), same jet canvas — only the colour roles change. Neon is kept on dark surfaces only. Pick a direction.

Option A · recommended

Red stays king — neon as supporting energy

Your brand doesn't change. Red is still the only CTA colour. Purple appears only as a soft background bloom + selected-state accent; lime green is reserved for Free / Live / ✓. Lowest risk, most cohesive with everything you already ship.

Option B · boldest

Red→purple gradient as the signature

Purple gets promoted to co-star via a red→purple gradient (they blend through magenta, so purple feels born from your red). The gradient becomes the brand signature on CTAs + headlines. Lime stays the single semantic pop. More distinctive, slightly more of a visual evolution.

Option C · biggest change

Purple primary, red demoted to alert/error

A real pivot: purple becomes the brand/CTA colour (community-app convention), green the action pop, and red retires to its semantic job (errors, destructive). Most "neon community" but it walks away from your current red identity — shown for contrast.
